Demystifying the Treasury Tech Market

Choosing treasury technology can feel overwhelming. We break the landscape into three clear categories so you can chart the right path forward.

1. Cash Tools

Cash Tools are the entry point to treasury technology. They give you a single place to see bank balances, manage simple forecasts, and handle routine transactions. Think of them as the digital version of a checkbookâ€”ideal for teams with straightforward cash needs.

For example, a regional manufacturer might use a Cash Tool to consolidate balances from a handful of banks and build a 13-week forecast.

2. TMS-Lite

TMS-Lite platforms add more horsepower with automated payments, bank connectivity, and approval workflows. They are perfect for growing companies that have outgrown spreadsheets but don't need every bell and whistle of a full-scale system. Picture upgrading from a sedan to a roomy SUV—more capacity without the complexity.

A fast-growing retailer expanding overseas might adopt a TMS-Lite solution to manage multiple bank portals and streamline daily payments.

3. TRMS

Treasury & Risk Management Systems (TRMS) deliver the most comprehensive functionality, from complex risk analytics to global cash pooling. These platforms are designed for enterprises with large international footprints and sophisticated treasury operations. Think of them as a fully loaded fleet ready for any journey.

A multinational corporation with dozens of entities relies on a TRMS to manage FX exposure, intercompany lending, and regulatory reporting in one place.

How the Categories Work Together

Many organizations combine elements from all three categories. Cash Tools handle daily visibility, a TMS-Lite platform automates payments, and a TRMS provides advanced risk management. The right mix depends on your cash complexity, geographic reach, and strategic goals.

When evaluating options, consider where you are today and where you want to be in the next few years. Our team can help you map a technology stack that grows alongside your business.
